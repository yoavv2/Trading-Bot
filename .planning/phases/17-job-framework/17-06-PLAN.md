## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| cancellation requester â†’ persisted audit record | `requested_by` is caller-supplied; in v1.x it is the single local operator, but the field shape must survive multi-user later |
| handler â†’ cancellation acknowledgement | A handler could otherwise claim cancellation that no operator requested |
| worker claim path â†” cancel path | A QUEUED Job could be claimed and cancelled concurrently, invoking a handler for a cancelled Job |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-17-06-01 | Repudiation | who cancelled a Job and why | mitigate | D-10 record persisted on both the Job row and an append-only `JobEvent`: `requested_by`, `reason`, `requested_at`, `acknowledged_at`, `terminal_cause`; a second request cannot overwrite the first requester |
| T-17-06-02 | Spoofing | handler fabricating an unrequested cancellation | mitigate | `acknowledge_cancellation` raises `JobNotCancellableError` unless `cancellation_requested_at` is already non-null |
| T-17-06-03 | Tampering | race between claim and cancel invoking a handler for a cancelled Job | mitigate | Both paths take `with_for_update=True` on the Job row; the cancel transaction and the claim transaction serialize, and CANCELLED is absorbing so a claim after cancel raises |
| T-17-06-04 | Spoofing | reporting a cancellation that did not actually stop external side effects | mitigate | D-09: grace-period overrun lands on FAILED with `cancellation_timeout` and `outcome_uncertain=True`, never on CANCELLED |
| T-17-06-05 | Spoofing | `requested_by` is unauthenticated in v1.x | accept | No auth surface ships in v1.x per REQUIREMENTS.md Out of Scope; the field is multi-user-shaped (AUD-03) so adding identity later requires no persistence redesign |
| T-17-06-SC | Tampering | package installs | accept | No new packages |
</threat_model>
